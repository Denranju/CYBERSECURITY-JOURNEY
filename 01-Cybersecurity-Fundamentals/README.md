# 📚 Topics Covered

## What is Cybersecurity?

Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting computers, networks, systems and digital information from unauthorized access, cyber attacks, damage or theft. It combines technology, processes and people to ensure systems remain secure.

---

## What is Information Security?

Information Security focuses on protecting information regardless of where it is stored. The primary goal is to maintain the confidentiality, integrity and availability of information.

---

## The CIA Triad

The CIA Triad is the foundation of information security.

### Confidentiality

Confidentiality ensures that sensitive information is only accessible to authorized individuals. Common methods used to maintain confidentiality include encryption, authentication and access control.

### Integrity

Integrity ensures that information remains accurate, complete and has not been altered without authorization. Hashing, digital signatures and version control help maintain integrity.

### Availability

Availability ensures that systems, services and data remain accessible whenever authorized users need them. Redundancy, backups and disaster recovery planning help maintain availability.

---

## Authentication

Authentication is the process of verifying the identity of a user, device or system before granting access.

Examples include:

- Passwords
- Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)
- Biometrics
- Security Tokens

---

## Authorization

Authorization determines what an authenticated user is allowed to access or perform after their identity has been verified.

---

## Accounting

Accounting records user activities and system events for auditing, monitoring and incident investigations.

---

## Threats

A threat is any event or action that could negatively impact the confidentiality, integrity or availability of information.

Examples include:

- Malware
- Phishing
- Insider Threats
- Denial-of-Service (DoS) Attacks
- Social Engineering

---

## Vulnerabilities

A vulnerability is a weakness in a system, application or process that could be exploited by an attacker.

Examples include:

- Weak passwords
- Unpatched software
- Misconfigured systems
- Outdated applications

---

## Risk

Risk is the likelihood that a threat will exploit a vulnerability and cause damage to an organization.

Organizations reduce risk by implementing appropriate security controls.

---

## Attack Surface

The attack surface represents all possible entry points an attacker could use to compromise a system or network.

Reducing the attack surface helps improve security.

---

## Security Controls

Security controls are safeguards implemented to reduce security risks.

Common security controls include:

- Firewalls
- Antivirus Software
- Encryption
- Access Control
- Security Policies
- Security Awareness Training

---

## Defense in Depth

Defense in Depth is a layered security strategy that uses multiple security controls to protect systems and data.

If one security control fails, additional layers continue to provide protection.

---

## Security Policies

Security policies define the rules and procedures organizations use to protect information and technology resources.

---

## Business Continuity

Business Continuity Planning ensures that an organization can continue operating during and after a security incident or disaster.

---

## Disaster Recovery

Disaster Recovery focuses on restoring systems, services and data after a major disruption.

---

## Incident Response

Incident Response is the structured process used to identify, contain, eradicate and recover from cybersecurity incidents.

Typical phases include:

1. Preparation
2. Identification
3. Containment
4. Eradication
5. Recovery
6. Lessons Learned

---

## Types of Hackers

Common categories include:

- White Hat Hackers
- Black Hat Hackers
- Grey Hat Hackers
- Script Kiddies
- Insider Threats
- Nation-State Actors
- Hacktivists

---

- Penetration Tester
- Incident Responder

- Security Engineer
- Malware Analyst
- Cloud Security Engineer

# ✅ Best Practices

Some important security best practices include:

- Use strong and unique passwords.
- Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA).
- Keep systems updated.
- Apply the principle of least privilege.
- Regularly back up important data.
- Monitor systems for suspicious activity.
- Educate users about phishing and social engineering.
- Follow organizational security policies.
